Friends - One of the most popular sitcoms ever aired

Friends is one of the most popular sitcoms of all time. It aired from 1994 to 2004. The show follows the lives of the six friends Monica Geller, Ross Geller, Chandler Bing, Joey Tribbiani, Rachel Green, and Phoebe Buffay, who live in Manhattan, New York City. The central premise of the show revolves around the group of friends navigating their personal and professional lives while sharing countless humorous and heartwarming moments. The series explores various themes such as friendship, love, career aspirations, and the challenges of adult life.

Our six lovely friends

The characters each have distinct personalities and storylines. Monica is known for her neatness and perfectionism, Ross for his intelligence and romantic misadventures, Chandler for his sarcastic humor, Joey for his acting career and womanizing tendencies, Rachel for her fashion sense and journey to independence, and Phoebe for her offbeat and eccentric nature.

20 years after the last episode - Still immense popularity

The sitcom became a cultural phenomenon and achieved immense popularity during its original run. It garnered a loyal fan base and received critical acclaim for its writing, ensemble cast performances, and relatable portrayal of young adult life. Even after the show ended, "Friends" has remained popular through syndication and streaming platforms, continuing to attract new generations of viewers.
